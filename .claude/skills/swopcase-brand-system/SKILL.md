# SwopCase Brand System

Use this skill whenever you design, refactor, or review any customer-facing part of the SwopCase storefront.

## Brand goal
SwopCase sells interchangeable phone case inserts ("Swops"). The store must make cold traffic instantly understand three things:
1. What the product is.
2. Why the store is trustworthy.
3. Why the product feels aspirational.

## Non-negotiables
- Website background base color is `#F1E9E9`.
- Do not propose changing the global background color.
- Product visuals must feel warm, feminine, clean, minimal, premium.
- Avoid over-relying on celebrity-brand assumptions; SwopCase must earn trust on-site.
- Never make the storefront feel like a generic AI template.
- Mobile-first always.

## Visual direction
- Clean Girl / It-Girl / ultra-feminine, but believable for a young ecommerce brand.
- Luxury comes from whitespace, restraint, consistency, hierarchy, and polish.
- Use soft contrast, soft shadows, subtle borders, rounded corners only where they feel premium.
- Prefer elegant restraint over flashy effects.

## Trust-first rule
For cold TikTok traffic, every major section should answer at least one of these:
- What is it?
- How does it work?
- Can I trust this store?
- Why is it aesthetically desirable?

If a section is beautiful but unclear, fix clarity first.

## Design tokens
- Background: `#F1E9E9`
- Supporting placeholder background: `#D9CDCD`
- Typography: clean sans-serif, minimal, modern, high readability
- Lighting language for visuals: warm soft side light, satin surfaces, clean minimal composition

## Layout rules
- Strong above-the-fold clarity.
- Clear CTA hierarchy.
- Generous spacing.
- Fast scanability on mobile.
- Avoid clutter, tiny text, dense icon walls, or overbuilt animations.

## Image rules
- Do not rely on full AI-generated faces in key hero visuals.
- Hands, product details, flatlays, cropped lifestyle scenes are preferred.
- UGC zones should feel more real than polished.
- If image requirements are unknown, define exact pixel dimensions before suggesting assets.

## Decision rubric
When reviewing a design choice, prioritize in this order:
1. Clarity
2. Trust
3. Conversion
4. Aesthetic desire
5. Novelty

## Output style
When making recommendations, be specific.
Prefer:
- exact section names
- exact spacing or hierarchy advice
- exact asset needs
- exact reasons tied to cold traffic conversion

Avoid vague praise or generic brand language.
